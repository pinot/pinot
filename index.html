Banana bread chia pudding

  • Time: 30 min (yes, you need to get up early to make this)
  • Serves: 2 (or 1 really hungry)
  • Calories: 495 kcal (should we call it 'brunch' instead?)
Ingredients Quantity
Chia pudding
non dairy milk 1 1/2 cup
ripe bananas 2
cinnamon and nutmeg 1/2 tsp
sugar/ sweetener as needed
salt a pinch
chia seeds 3 or 4 tbsp
Caramelised bananas
ripe bananas sliced 2
vegan brown sugar 2 tbsp
non dairy milk 2 tbsp
cinammon or nutmeg a pinch

Step 1: Blend all the ingredients under chia pudding except chia seeds until smooth. Or mash the banana and mix in with the rest of the ingredients. Bring the mixture to a boil over medium heat then take off heat. (I like to cook the mixture so the banana flavor intensifies and adds a more baked/caramelized banana bread flavor.).

Step 2: Mix in the chia seeds into the hot mixture and let sit. Whisk again after 5 minutes then chill for atleast 15 minutes before layering. (Chia seeds can be mixed into the mixture without boiling as well. Mix in, refrigerate and mix after 15 mins to distribute the seeds well. Chill for a few hours to hydrate before layering).

Step 3: Caramelize the bananas: Combine banana slices, sugar, non dairy milk, nutmeg and a pinch of salt in a skillet over medium heat. Cook until the mixture thickens, stirring occasionally. about 5 minutes.

Step 4: Layer the banana chia pudding in jars with toasted walnuts, caramelized bananas and flaked coconut. Top with some coconut flakes and a sprinkle of cinnamon or cardamom.

Spicy avocado chickpea salad sandwich

  • Time: 25 min (easy chick-peasy)
  • Serves: 2 (st Valentine is on)
  • Calories: 364 kcal (yes, dessert is on the way)
Ingredients Quantity
ripe avocado 1
cooked/drained chickpeas 1 1/2 cup
chopped red onion 1/4 cup
chopped tomato (optional) 1/4 cup
green chili finely chopped 1
cilantro chopped 1/4 cup
ground cumin 1/2 tsp
garam masala powder generous pinch
limon juice 1 lime
black pepper a good pinch
salt as needed
greens, tomatoes, lettuce
any kind of bread you fancy

Step 1: In a bowl, mash the chickpeas and avocado until avocado is well mashed and most of the chickpeas are not whole.

Step 2: Add all the ingredients from onion through spices. Mix in. Taste and adjust salt and spice.

Step 3: Prep the sandwich bread by toasting if needed. Add a layer of greens or micro-greens of choice. Layer a good helping of the smashed chickpea avocado mixture.

Step 4: Add a layer of juicy tomatoes or sprouts and serve. Use this mash in sandwiches, tacos, wraps or salad bowl. Change up the spices for variation.
